Cards

Payment cards are a popular method of payment for both online and in-person transactions. They include credit cards, debit cards, and prepaid cards. Credit cards allow consumers to borrow money from the card issuer and pay it back over time, usually with interest. Debit cards allow consumers to spend money they already have in their bank account, while prepaid cards are loaded with a certain amount of money and can be used until the balance is depleted.​

Bank Debits

Bank debits allow consumers to pay for goods and services directly from their bank account. This can be done through a variety of methods, including online banking, mobile banking, and automatic payments. Bank debits are often used for recurring payments, such as utility bills and subscription services.​

Buy Now Pay Later

Buy Now Pay Later (BNPL) is a relatively new payment method that allows consumers to purchase goods and services and pay for them over time. Typically, BNPL services offer interest-free financing for a certain period of time, such as six months or a year. After the interest-free period ends, interest is charged on the remaining balance.​

eWallets / Digital Wallets

eWallets, also known as digital wallets, are online accounts that allow consumers to store payment information and make purchases without the need to enter their payment details each time. Popular eWallets include PayPal, Apple Pay, and Google Wallet. They are often used for online transactions but can also be used in-store through mobile devices.​

Over-the-Counter

Over-the-Counter (OTC) payments are made in-person at a physical location, such as a convenience store or a bank. OTC payments typically involve cash or a check, but some services also accept debit or credit cards.​

Crypto

Cryptocurrencies, such as Bitcoin and Ethereum, are digital assets that can be used to purchase goods and services. They are decentralized, meaning they are not controlled by any government or financial institution. Crypto payments are typically made through a digital wallet and require the use of a unique address to send and receive funds.​

3-D Secure Processing

3-D Secure Processing is a security protocol designed to protect against fraudulent online transactions. It requires the cardholder to enter a password or a one-time code, which is sent to their mobile device, before the transaction can be completed. This adds an extra layer of security and helps prevent unauthorized use of credit and debit cards.​

eWallet Processing

eWallet Processing is a security feature that allows consumers to store their payment information in an encrypted digital wallet. This eliminates the need to enter payment details for each transaction, reducing the risk of sensitive information being intercepted or stolen.​

Currency Processing

Currency Processing is a feature that allows businesses to accept payments in multiple currencies. This is useful for online businesses that sell products or services to customers in different countries. Currency Processing automatically converts payments into the merchant's preferred currency, making it easier to manage international transactions.​

Fraud Control

Fraud Control is a set of security measures designed to prevent fraudulent transactions. This includes features such as real-time fraud detection, card verification, and transaction monitoring. Fraud Control helps to protect both merchants and consumers from financial losses due to fraudulent activity.​

Card Tokenization

Card Tokenization is a security feature that replaces sensitive payment information, such as credit card numbers, with a unique token. The token can be used for transactions without revealing the actual card details, reducing the risk of sensitive information being stolen or intercepted.​

API Agent

An API Agent is a security feature that controls access to a payment gateway through an Application Programming Interface (API). This helps to prevent unauthorized access to the payment gateway and provides an additional layer of security for online transactions.​
